About me

Hi! I'm Arsalan, a computer vision researcher trying to teach machines to see the world in ways humans can't. I grew up fascinated by flight and earned my aerospace engineering degree (with a gold medal!). But I became even more captivated by how we can use cameras and algorithms to perceive and help machines understand the world which sometimes somethings are invisible to our eyes - from detecting early bruises under apple skin before they're visible, to helping doctors see inside the human body in with AI based medical imaging, to assisting cultural heritage professionals digitize and understand historical artifacts using Computer Vision. Now I'm completing my PhD across two countries (Norway and France), working on AI systems that capture and model how light interacts with surfaces. My research has taken me from Viking-age textiles in Oslo museums to operating rooms in France, always asking: "How can we make machines see better?" I love projects where computer vision meets real-world messiness: autonomous robots navigating unknown environments, sensors struggling with weird lighting, defects hiding just below the surface. The gap between "works in the lab" and "works on the runway" is where things get interesting. Currently I am based in the beautiful small town of Gjøvik in Norway, where I balance PhD thesis writing with padel playing and walking along beautiful lake of Mjøsa. Always happy to chat about computer vision, robotics, or why hyperspectral/multispectral imaging is cooler than it sounds. Looking forward for new opportunities to collaborate on interesting projects that life will bring to me
